What is the difference between *knowledge* and *information*?

Information is often easily summarized in one sentence or less, you could say it is **dead**, information is functionally **dead** when speaking of intellectual processes.

> At 01:15 I, EB, write a note about knowledge v. information

What do you do with that knowledge? 
Nothing.
It is **dead**.
There's nowhere to go from that sentence.

*So what do I do?*
Enrich the infomation you come across, plug it with context and relevance and other related things.
All knowledge you add to your notes has the potential to be useful in the future!
Don't stick to any one project at a time, take broad notes and focus on your current projects; follow the path outlined by your projects and deviate a little. You'll be surprised the ideas that appear that way!
