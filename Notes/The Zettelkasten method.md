
> [!quote] On notes - Niklas Luhmann
> What are we to do with what we have written down? Certainly, at first, we will produce mostly garbage. But we have been educated to expect something useful from our activities and soon lose confidence if nothing useful seems to result. We should, therefore, reflect on whether and how we arrange our notes so that they are available for later access.

---
[Niklas Luhmann](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Niklas_Luhmann) is the *godfather of the Zettelkasten method*.
He was a social scientist who wrote and published 50 books and over 600 articles, with many more left after his death.

How in the hell did he achieve such efficiency?

The **Zettelkasten** (lit. *note box*), also referred to as the **second brain** is a collection of notes with *hypertext* designed to connect to each other and make navigation between them as painless as possible.

- New insights never happen in a vaccum, we need to make *unexpected connections* to make them
- The method aims to *reduce friction* by giving clear guidelines on what to do
- *No effort is wasted*, even if you don't need the info you're currently making a note of, that knowledge may come in useful for future endeavours!
- *Break complex problems down* by working on a topics atomic principles
- The system *adapts to the volume of information*, it doesn't cause bloat
- Enables writing to become *easier, more coherent, smoother and convincing* since it connects various lines of thought across different notes


> [!todo|noicon] Anatomy of a **Zettel**

1. A unique ID: Can be the date, an arbitrary numbering system or simply the name of the Zettel
2. The body: The actual meat of what you're trying to write about, *written in your own words*. No copy pasting, since that doesn't force you to think about what you're writing about. Writing the things yourself helps with *recalling the information* and actually understanding the material you're reading. The *Zettelkasten* shines when the thoughts are yours.
The length of the note depends on what you're trying to achieve, it is generally recommended to write a single thought per Zettel, remember, it's supposed to be atomic.
The Zettelkasten is supposed to assist you with *thinking*, not creating *excerpts*. Stick to [[Knowledge instead of information]]

3. References: The sources of the information, sometimes encoded, sometimes not. You can link the web, books and even other notes.
